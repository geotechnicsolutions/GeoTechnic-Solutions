What Are Rare Earth Elements?

Rare Earth Elements (REEs) are a group of 17 chemical elements essential to modern technologies: 15 lanthanides plus scandium and yttrium. They are critical for the development of clean energy technologies, electric vehicles, wind turbines, permanent magnets, and advanced electronics.

High-Demand REEs (2025–2035)

  • Neodymium (Nd)
    Used in permanent magnets for EVs and turbines. More
  • Praseodymium (Pr)
    Magnet alloys and aerospace. More
  • Dysprosium (Dy)
    High-temp magnets for EV motors. More
  • Terbium (Tb)
    Enhances magnetic performance. More
  • Samarium (Sm)
    Lasers, radar, magnets. More
  • Yttrium (Y)
    LEDs, superconductors, ceramics. More
  • Europium (Eu)
    Phosphors, quantum devices. More
  • Cerium (Ce)
    Catalysts and polishing. More
  • Lanthanum (La)
    Batteries and optical glass. More
